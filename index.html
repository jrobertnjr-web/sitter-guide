🌡️ Heat is the #1 danger — read first

French Bulldogs are flat-faced and overheat fast. In a Texas summer this can turn into an emergency in minutes.

  • Walk only in the cool hours — before ~9 AM and after ~8 PM. Keep it short.
  • Never leave them outside unattended in the sun, and never in a parked car — not even for a minute.
  • Keep the AC running. A cool tile floor and fresh water are their friends.
  • Snorting and the occasional honking "reverse sneeze" are normal for the breed — don't panic.
  • Heavy raspy breathing, bright-red or bluish gums, heavy drooling, or wobbliness are NOT normal → see the SOS tab.

                  🔥 Signs of heatstroke (most common Frenchie emergency)

                  • Frantic, loud, raspy panting that won't settle
                  • Thick drool, bright-red or bluish gums/tongue
                  • Glazed eyes, stumbling, weakness, vomiting, collapse

                  If a dog is overheating

                  1
                  Get them to shade or AC immediately.
                  2
                  Cool with cool (not ice-cold) water on belly, paws & armpits. Aim a fan at them.
                  3
                  Offer small sips of water — never force it.
                  4
                  Call the vet and head in. Heatstroke needs a vet even if they seem to recover.

                  If a dog is choking

                  Frenchies are greedy eaters. If one is pawing at the mouth, gagging, or can't breathe: open the mouth and look for an object you can sweep out with a finger. If you can't clear it, get to the vet fast — call on the way.

                  Possible poisoning

                  If a dog eats something it shouldn't (chocolate, grapes/raisins, xylitol gum, medication, etc.), call the vet or ASPCA Animal Poison Control: (888) 426-4435 right away. Note what and how much.
